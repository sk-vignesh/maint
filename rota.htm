Week 1 Shifts may change throughout the week due to factors such as holidays, sickness, driver fatigue, or other operational requirements. As a result, some shifts may need to be brought forward or adjusted at short notice.
We remain mindful of drivers’ legal working time limits, the potential for shift extensions, and the effect this can have on required rest periods.
Week 2 Some shift start times have been pushed later, as indicated by the red markings. This requires careful management, as it may necessitate reassigning drivers or reducing their legal rest periods. Failure to monitor this closely can result in cumulative impacts on later shifts, particularly where delays, overruns, or poor time management occur.
Other Considerations Another key consideration is vehicle movements, which must also be factored into driver working time and rota planning. These are scheduled on an ad-hoc basis, often with only a few days’ notice, depending on workshop availability.
Carriers are not permitted to use agency drivers unless they have already been fully onboarded into the RELAY system, including completing background checks and drug and alcohol testing.
If rota or driver management issues arise and we are unable to cover a shift, we may be forced to drop a tour. This results in financial and operational impacts for the customer, Amazon, and the carrier.
